Women with Baskets

Anita Magsaysay-Ho (1914–2012) was one of the Philippines' most celebrated modernist painters. Known for her distinctive style blending cubist influences with Filipino folk themes, she is best remembered for her iconic depictions of women engaged in everyday tasks — market vendors, fisherwomen, and farmers. Her work captures the grace and quiet dignity of the Filipino woman, rendered in warm earthy tones and bold, geometric forms. Magsaysay-Ho was the only female member of the Thirteen Moderns, a pioneering group of Filipino artists in the 1930s.
